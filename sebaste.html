Sebaste: A Grand Staircase into the Wild.

Sebaste, Antique(207KM) is a unprepossessing jewel of a community. While Sebaste is the locationof Igpasungaw Falls and certainly other attractions, for me the charm was more pervasive. While the Municipal Building is just off the National Highway, the church and plaza are well off the highway forming a clean, unspoiled and peaceful center to the town. It's only steps from the church to the sea. The entire tableau was charming. When I was there the church plaza was filled with school children. The streets and plaza were clean and well kept.

These stunning works of Mother Nature speak volumes of beauty, allure, and power. They are venues for heart-stopping adventures, sanctuaries for serenity, and sources for a land's commerce and survival. Thus, when we knew that one of our destinations during our Experience Antique familiarization tour is the magnificient Igpasungaw Falls in the municipality of Sebaste, our hearts beat with excitement and delight.

Mountains and Expansive Rice Fields

  1. Sebaste, like the rest of Antique, is incredibly green and unspoiled. Check out those lovely mountains, expansive rice fields, and thick woods. No wonder the air here is cool and refreshing
  2. The sound of a powerful river serenaded us as we continued hiking the easy trail. Isn't that gorgeous? With the heat becoming more intense by the minute, we found it very hard to resist the temptation to simply jump into the clean water. This river we failed to ask the guides what its name is seems to be the source of patable water in Sebaste due to the presence of large pipes and humming pumps.
  3. We were intrigued and concerned when we saw a sign that said "Washing Area." We truly hope that no one actually washes clothes here. Not only is the area dangerous for washing, but laundering clothes here can also severly damage the river. Detergents are polluntants that can produce an overabundance of algae, which suffocates the river.
  4. When we arrived at Igpatuyaw, the entire sitio was quite as people went inside their homes tp watch noontime shows and take refuge from the noon sun's heat. This old woman however snubbed the onslaught of the fiery ball in the sky while tending to rice gains being dried under the sun. Once completely dried, the grains are then taken to the mill.

Pandan: The Hidden Jewel of Antique.

A major tourist destination for its natural wonders, Pandan caters to those who love nature and the outdoors. The third-class municipality is one of the Antique's industrialized towns and is also home to several beaches located along the Pandan Bay. So if you're looking to spend a fun day in the sun, this lovely town in Antique should be on your bucket list.

The municipality of Pandan, at the northern end of the province, is home to the clear, turquoise waters of Malumpati Cold Spring, a popular weekend destination among locals. Malumpati's waters flow all the way to the Bugang River, another popular destination in town

Glide along the mystical river of Bugang

  1. Aboard bamboo rafts, visitors will cruise along the crystal clear waters of the Bugang River, considered one of the Philippines' cleanest bodies of water. The cruise passes through five barangays and along a dense forest and sparse villages. Overrun by vegetation at the banks of the river are old, chipped statues of a woman flanked by two crocodiles on either side. This statue represents the local legend of Inday Gamay, a woman who was the subject of a fierce battle between crocodiles Manunggong and Mandurriao, both of whom died fighting in the Bugang riverbank where Inday Gamay is said to have been hidden.

There are several groups and barangays participating wearing uniforms or dressing up like natives, the "Atis", in honor of one of the ancestors of the Pandananons aside from the Indo-Malays, Chinese, and Spaniards. In year 2002, the festival was called Mantiw-Mantiwan Festival which included Ati-Atihan and Mardi-Gras Competition.
